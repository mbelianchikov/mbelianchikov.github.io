Friday evening fun

This page is about “Friday evening” experiments with undergrad students or other things that I found amusing.

Paul trap: two spoons and copper ring in between powered by 2kV 50Hz AC from microwave oven transformer form hyperbolic rotating electric field. Such field configuration delivers potential minima for dielectric particles. The video was recorded by strobing the highlight LED by AC generator at 48.910 Hz.


21 cm radiotelescope: first run. Radiotelescope based on SDR receiver (RSP1A), G4DDK low noise amplifier with 37 dB gain and 0.3 dB noise figure and horn antenna calculated for 20 dBi gain. The horn antenna design was chosen to be close to original work of H. Ewen and E. Purcell about observation of the Galactic hydrogen [Ewen, H. I.; Purcell, E. M. Nature, Volume 168, Issue 4270, pp. 356 (1951)]. Emission of neutral hydrogen experienced a doppler shift in different arms of Milky Way can be seen on the graph.


DIY SiC LED. 30V DC voltage applied to a SiC crystal. The first LED ever accidently discovered by prof. N. Losev In Nizhniy Novgorod. A brilliant scientist with a tragic fate.


Leaflet distribution created by alternating double side wind. The good example of something very simple by nature but very tricky to describe analytically.


Isopropanol cloud chamber for cosmic ray detection cooled with liquid N2. Evaporating N2 partially solves the problem of fogging of the chamber walls, but dry ice gives a more stable performance.


EuRuFe4As4 crystal covered with gold after FTIR measurement with gold evaporation technique.


Thermoluminescence of fluorite. A natural crystal heated on an electric stove starts to glow when created by natural radiation trapped electronic states are released. Thermoluminescence is the primary dating method for non-carbon materials.


Levitation of neodymium magnet between two diamagnetic Bi ingots.


Natural snowflakes preserved in cyanoacrylate. Better to use two acrylic plates instead of a slide glass and a cover slip since cyanoacrylate do not bond well with smooth glass and after month or so coverslip peels off (see left picture).


NH4 water solution in diamond anvil cell, 3.6 GPa on the left and 62 GPa on the right, respectively.
