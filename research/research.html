Current research

    My current research interests and my PhD thesis are focused on physics of dipolar matter. Fascinating phenomena of magnetism amazes by the vast plurality of phases generated from the elementary magnetic moment by interactions at different energy scales. Lying at the very top of the hierarchy of interactions dipole-dipole interaction (DDI) stabilizes the most subtle exotic magnetic phases and inspires for search of analogues phases in the “electric” dipolar world. While low energy and long-range nature of the DDI interaction brings a challenge both to experimenters and theoreticians, it is a new frontier on the map of phase diagrams where new fascinating phases could be discovered.

    In my PhD thesis I focused on electric dipolar lattices of polar molecules in the matrix of dielectric crystals. By the mean of broad set of spectroscopic techniques, we explored a vast area of the phase diagram along a temperature axis and discovered three new phases of electric dipolar lattice of water molecules: quantum paraelectric, dipolar glass and nontrivial ordered phase.
PhD thesis: Phase states of an electric dipole lattice of water molecules in a matrix of beryl and cordierite crystals (In Russian, Eng. soon)

Dipolar lattice in beryl: quantum paraelectricity

The localization of water molecules in the structural channels of a beryl single crystal suppress hydrogen bonding, but remain all degree of freedom and a strong dipolar coupling, thus allowing to build robust and near perfect triangular dipolar lattice. In the wide temperature range paraelectric behavior was observed with suppression of a transition to ordered state due to quantum fluctuations.
Nature Communications 7, 12842 (2016)


Dipolar lattice in cordierite: nontrivial dipolar order

Similar to beryl, cordierite crystal presents nanopores but featured with orthorhombic symmetry. Dipolar lattice of water molecules localized in a cordierite inherits crystal symmetry and the lower orthorhombic symmetry instigate a phase transition to nontrivial ordered state: planes with ferroelectric order alternate in antiferroelectric way.
Nature Communications 11, 3927 (2020)


Water-II: distribution study

Among with freely rotating water-I molecules in beryl and cordierite crystals there are water molecules of a different flavor: strongly correlated by alkali ions water-II molecules are introduce polarization centers which affect on whole electric dipolar lattice. IR Mapping of synthetic beryl crystals with D2O molecules revealed uniform distribution of Water-I molecules and highly heterogeneous distribution of Water-II. Observed heterogeneity is explained by shift in isomorphic substitution equation due to elevated internal pressure at the boundaries of the growth sectors.
Crystal Growth & Design 21, 2283 (2021)

Past research and side projects

EuRbFe4As4: «ferromagnetic superconductor»

Unique interplay between superconducting and ferromagnetic orders is observed in iron-pnictide EuRuFeAs4 by the mean of transport, magnetic, and optical study. Surprisingly weak effect on the superconducting condensate has been observed upon magnetic ordering, indicating a rather weak interaction between Eu and Fe sublattices.
Physical Review B 98, 140506(R) (2018)


H2O@C60: coherent rotation motion

Real-time coherent rotational motion of isolated water molecules encapsulated in fullerene-C60 cages resolved by time-domain terahertz spectroscopy (TDS THz). We employ single-cycle THz pulses to excite the low-frequency rotational motion of water and measure the subsequent coherent emission of electromagnetic waves by water molecules.
Scientific Reports 10, 18329 (2020)


Carbon nanotube network: electrical transport mechanism

Implementation of single-walled carbon nanotubes (SWCNT) films for micro-electronic devices strongly motivates studies of their electrical and optical Here, we have performed a comprehensive broad-band spectroscopic investigation of high-quality pristine and doped (iodine, CuCl) SWCNT films. Typical metallic-like behavior of the conductivity has been discovered and, applying Drude conductivity model, we have determined the temperature and doping dependences of effective parameters of the charge carriers: plasma frequency, scattering rate, mobility, mean-free path.
Carbon 126, 544 (2018)


Graphene|insulator|grating: plasmon enhanced terahertz detection

Transistor structure comprising CVD graphene and subwavelength metal gratings was fabricated to enhance plasmon resonance in graphene and hence overcome the low electromagnetic wave absorbance of a single sheet of graphene. The plasmon resonance in the structure was clearly observed with the plasmon lifetime exceeding the transport relaxation time, in contrast to reports for encapsulated graphene. We find that a grating coupler close to graphene strongly modifies the plasmon spectrum, which is determined by metal stripe width but not by grating period.
Physical Review Applied 11, 054017 (2019)

\(\boldsymbol{ B^{0}_s \rightarrow \overline{K}^{\star}(892)^0 K^{\star}(892)^0} \): beauty and strange

My bachelor project devoted to the analysis of a data from LHCb detector (LHC CERN). Utilizing standard HEP approaches, the decay cross section of the \(B^{0}_s\) meson into the new \(\overline{K}^{\star}(892)^0 K^{\star}(892)^0\) channel has been defined as a ratio to the known \( \Psi(2S)\phi \) reference channel.
